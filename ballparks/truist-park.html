What Makes Truist Park Special

Truist Park does something no other ballpark in Major League Baseball has pulled off quite as well: it turned the entire surrounding area into the attraction. The Battery Atlanta, a sprawling 1.5-million-square-foot mixed-use entertainment district, wraps around the ballpark on three sides. Restaurants, bars, shops, a concert venue, a movie theater, a hotel -- all of it steps from the gates and open year-round. On game days, The Battery fills up hours before first pitch and stays alive long after the final out. The idea was that attending a Braves game should feel like an entire evening out, not just a three-hour sit in a seat, and they nailed it.

Inside the park, the design blends modern comforts with a distinctly Southern warmth. The concourses are wide and open, with sightlines to the field from almost everywhere. The Hank Aaron Terrace in the outfield offers a social gathering space with standing room, group areas, and premium food options -- all with a view of the game. It is named, of course, for the all-time great who spent the prime of his career as a Brave and broke Babe Ruth's home run record in Atlanta in 1974.

Beyond the outfield, Monument Garden honors the greatest players in franchise history with bronze sculptures and detailed plaques. Walking through it, you feel the weight of a franchise that spans three cities -- Boston, Milwaukee, and Atlanta -- and more than a century of baseball. Hank Aaron, Eddie Mathews, Warren Spahn, Greg Maddux, Tom Glavine, John Smoltz, Chipper Jones -- the names read like a Hall of Fame all on their own.

And then there is 2021. The Braves won the World Series that year, bringing a championship back to Atlanta for the first time since 1995. Truist Park, barely four years old at the time, became the stage for one of the most dramatic postseason runs in recent memory. The gold-trimmed World Series banners and the trophy display inside the park are a reminder that this is not just a pretty venue -- it is a place where history was made.

Local Travel & Nearby Attractions

Truist Park sits in Cobb County, northwest of downtown Atlanta, but the metro area is packed with world-class destinations worth building a trip around.

The Battery Atlanta

Adjacent · Entertainment & Dining

The Battery is the mixed-use entertainment district that surrounds Truist Park on three sides. It features more than two dozen restaurants and bars, retail shops, the Coca-Cola Roxy concert venue with a 3,800-person capacity, a luxury movie theater, and a full-service hotel. Live music and events run before and after games throughout the season. Arrive early and plan to spend time here -- it is the centerpiece of the Truist Park experience and open year-round, not just on game days.

Georgia Aquarium

15 miles · Family & Nature

The largest aquarium in the Western Hemisphere, home to whale sharks, beluga whales, manta rays, and thousands of other marine animals. The Ocean Voyager exhibit features a massive acrylic tunnel that lets you walk beneath whale sharks gliding overhead. Allow at least three hours for a full visit. Located in downtown Atlanta near Centennial Olympic Park.

Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park

15 miles · History & Culture

A National Park Service site preserving the birthplace, childhood home, and final resting place of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. The park includes Ebenezer Baptist Church, where King and his father preached, the King Center, and a powerful walking tour through the Sweet Auburn neighborhood that shaped the civil rights movement. Free admission. A must-visit for any trip to Atlanta.

World of Coca-Cola

15 miles · Museum & Experience

An interactive museum dedicated to the history and global reach of Coca-Cola, which was invented in Atlanta in 1886. The highlight is the tasting room, where you can sample more than 100 Coca-Cola beverages from around the world -- some delicious, some memorably strange. Located in downtown Atlanta next to the Georgia Aquarium, making it easy to pair both in a single day.

Piedmont Park & Atlanta BeltLine

12 miles · Park & Urban Trail

Piedmont Park is Atlanta's signature green space -- 200 acres of lawns, trails, and skyline views in the heart of Midtown. The Atlanta BeltLine, a 22-mile multi-use trail built on former railroad corridors, connects Piedmont Park to neighborhoods across the city with walking and biking paths, public art installations, and a string of restaurants and breweries along the route. Rent a bike or scooter and explore.

Getting There

MARTA Shuttle

Truist Park does not have direct MARTA rail access -- the ballpark is in Cobb County, outside the rail system's reach. On game days, the Braves operate a shuttle bus from the Arts Center MARTA station (Red and Gold lines) to The Battery. The shuttle runs before and after games for a small fee. It is the best public transit option, but plan for extra travel time, especially on weekend games when demand is high.

Driving

Most fans drive to Truist Park. The stadium is located at the intersection of I-285 and I-75, two of the busiest interstate highways in the Southeast. On game days, traffic on both interstates can be brutal -- allow significantly more time than your GPS suggests. Approach from I-285 West when possible to avoid the worst of the I-75 northbound congestion.

Parking

Truist Park offers ample parking in structured garages and surface lots surrounding The Battery. Prices vary by proximity, with most lots running $20 to $40 on game days. Pre-purchasing a parking pass through the Braves app or a third-party parking service is strongly recommended -- it saves time and guarantees a spot. Once you park in The Battery area, everything is walkable.

Rideshare

Uber and Lyft have designated drop-off and pickup zones near The Battery. After the game, expect surge pricing and significant waits as thousands of fans request rides at the same time. If you can linger at a Battery restaurant for 30 to 45 minutes after the final out, the surge subsides and the pickup experience improves dramatically.

Fan Tips

Arrive Early to Explore The Battery

The Battery Atlanta is half the experience. Arrive at least two hours before first pitch to walk the district, grab a pre-game meal, and enjoy the atmosphere. The restaurants and bars fill up fast on weekend games, so earlier is better. Live music often plays on the outdoor stages before the gates open, giving the whole area a festival energy that you will miss if you show up at game time.

Waffle House Inside the Park

Yes, really -- there is a Waffle House concession stand inside Truist Park, the only one in any MLB stadium. Located on the first-base side, it serves a limited menu of Waffle House classics including their signature waffles and hash browns. For anyone from the South, it is practically a pilgrimage. Lines get long, especially in the middle innings, so visit early or late for a shorter wait.

Chop House for Premium Views

The Chop House is a full-service restaurant built into the left field area of Truist Park with floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the field. You can watch the game while eating a sit-down meal -- steaks, burgers, craft cocktails -- without leaving your table. Reservations fill up quickly on popular game days, so book well in advance through the Braves website. Even if you do not have a reservation, the standing area near the Chop House offers some of the best casual viewing spots in the park.

Honest Drawbacks

Suburban Location

Truist Park is in Cobb County, not in Atlanta proper. Unlike ballparks embedded in city neighborhoods -- Wrigley in Wrigleyville, Fenway in the Fenway-Kenmore neighborhood -- Truist Park sits in a suburban area off the interstate. The Battery does an impressive job creating a walkable environment once you arrive, but getting there requires a car or rideshare for most visitors. There is no urban neighborhood to wander through, no side streets full of dive bars and local character. The vibe, for better or worse, is more curated than organic.

No Direct MARTA Access

The lack of direct MARTA rail service is the single biggest complaint about Truist Park. Fans staying in downtown Atlanta or near the airport cannot hop on a train and walk to the game the way you can at most urban ballparks. The shuttle from Arts Center station helps, but it adds time and complexity. For a metro area the size of Atlanta, not having rail access to the ballpark is a significant drawback.

Interstate Traffic

I-75 and I-285 are notoriously congested highways on a normal day. On game days, the traffic around Truist Park can be genuinely brutal, especially for weeknight games when rush hour and the pre-game influx collide. Budget an extra 30 to 60 minutes beyond what your GPS tells you, and leave early. Post-game traffic getting out of the parking garages and back onto the interstate can take just as long.

The Battery Can Feel Like a Mall

The Battery Atlanta is polished, well-maintained, and full of options -- but some fans find it feels more like an upscale outdoor shopping mall than a genuine neighborhood. Everything was built at once, by one developer, with a cohesive corporate design. It lacks the grit, history, and randomness of an actual city neighborhood surrounding a ballpark. If you prefer the authenticity of Wrigleyville or the chaos of Lansdowne Street outside Fenway, The Battery may feel a bit too sanitized.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is The Battery Atlanta at Truist Park?

The Battery Atlanta is a 1.5-million-square-foot mixed-use entertainment district that surrounds Truist Park on three sides. It includes more than two dozen restaurants and bars, retail shops, a movie theater, the Coca-Cola Roxy concert venue, a full-service hotel, and residential spaces. Unlike standalone ballpark neighborhoods that evolved over decades, The Battery was purpose-built alongside the stadium and is open year-round, not just on game days. It is designed so fans can arrive hours before first pitch, eat, shop, and enjoy live entertainment all within walking distance of the gates.

Can you take MARTA to Truist Park?

There is no direct MARTA rail station at Truist Park. The ballpark is located in Cobb County, outside the MARTA rail system's service area. However, on game days the Braves operate a shuttle bus from the Arts Center MARTA station (on the Red and Gold lines) directly to The Battery. The shuttle runs before and after games and costs a small fee. You can also take MARTA to other stations and use a rideshare for the last few miles. Without MARTA rail access, most fans drive or use rideshare services to reach the park.

Is there a Waffle House inside Truist Park?

Yes, there really is a Waffle House inside Truist Park. Located on the first-base side of the park, it is the only Waffle House operating inside a Major League Baseball stadium. It serves a limited menu of Waffle House classics, including their signature waffles, hash browns, and other breakfast items, during Braves games. For many fans, especially those from the South where Waffle House is a cultural institution, it is one of the most memorable concession stands in all of baseball. Lines can be long, so visit early if you want the full experience.
